# Past Submission Rejection Reasons — Case Library
# Use these as examples to recognize similar patterns in new submissions

## Case 1 — SUB-2023-004421 | REJECTED
Organization: QuickCover Health LLC (ORG-HC-55501)
Risk Score: 91 | Decision: AUTO_REJECT
Reason: All 4 plans had identical premiums ($299/mo) regardless of plan type (HMO vs PPO)
        and coverage area (California vs Nevada). Actuarially impossible.
Lesson: Identical premiums across different plan types in different states = strong fraud indicator.

## Case 2 — SUB-2023-007832 | REJECTED
Organization: Sunset Premier Plans (ORG-HC-33287)
Risk Score: 84 | Decision: AUTO_REJECT
Reason: Bronze HMO premium of $89/month for California — 80% below regional benchmark.
        No actuarial filing provided. State license listed as "pending."
Lesson: Premium below $200 in California for any non-catastrophic plan is not credible.

## Case 3 — SUB-2023-011045 | REJECTED
Organization: (First-time submitter)
Risk Score: 78 | Decision: AUTO_REJECT
Reason: Out-of-pocket maximum ($1,200) was LOWER than the deductible ($3,500).
        This is mathematically impossible — member would owe more than the OOP cap.
Lesson: Always check OOP max >= deductible. This is a hard reject rule.

## Case 5 — SUB-2024-000312 | APPROVED after HUMAN_REVIEW
Organization: New entrant — MountainView Health Co.
Risk Score: 52 | Decision: HUMAN_REVIEW → APPROVED
Reason: Premium was 15% above regional benchmark (unusual but not disqualifying).
        Senior reviewer verified actuarial filing justified the higher premium.
Lesson: Above-benchmark premiums are not automatically fraud — justify with actuarial data.

## Case 6 — SUB-2024-001891 | REJECTED
Organization: (First-time submitter — "USA Best Plans Inc.")
Risk Score: 95 | Decision: AUTO_REJECT
Reason: Coverage area listed as all 50 states. License was for Florida only.
        Network size claimed 2,000,000 providers (more than exist in the US).
        Contact email was a Gmail address.
