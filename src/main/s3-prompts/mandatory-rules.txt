MANDATORY COMPLIANCE RULES (Federal/State Requirements)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These rules are derived from federal and state regulations and MUST be enforced strictly.

1. ORGANIZATION IDENTIFICATION
   ❌ REJECT if: Organization ID does not follow format "ORG-HC-#####" (5 digits)
   Regulation: CMS Provider Identification Standard (45 CFR 162)

2. STATE LICENSING
   ❌ REJECT if: State license number is missing, invalid, or listed as "pending" or "not issued"
   Regulation: State Insurance Commissioner requirements (varies by state)

3. CONTACT INFORMATION
   ❌ REJECT if: Email address is invalid (missing @ symbol or domain like .com, .org)
   ❌ REJECT if: Phone number is obviously fake (e.g., 555-0000, all zeros)
   Regulation: CMS Contact Information Requirements

4. ATTESTATION
   ❌ REJECT if: Attestation signature is missing or listed as "not provided"
   Regulation: ACA Section 1311 - Certification Requirements

5. PREMIUM LIMITS (Hard Boundaries)
   ❌ REJECT if: Premium is less than $50/month (financially non-viable, fraud risk)
   ❌ REJECT if: Premium is more than $5000/month (unreasonable, potential fraud)
   Regulation: Market conduct standards

6. DEDUCTIBLE LIMITS (ACA Compliance)
   ❌ REJECT if: Deductible exceeds $9,450/year for individual plans (2024 ACA limit)
   ❌ REJECT if: Deductible is $0 while premium is suspiciously low (fraud indicator)
   Regulation: ACA Section 1302 - Cost-sharing limits

7. REQUIRED FIELDS
   ❌ REJECT if ANY of these are missing:
   - Organization Name
   - Primary Contact Name
   - Contact Email
   - At least one plan with: Name, Type, Premium, Deductible
   - Compliance Information section
   - Attestation section
   Regulation: CMS Submission Requirements

8. PREVIOUS COMPLIANCE HISTORY
   ❌ REJECT if: 50% or more previous submissions were rejected AND there is a pending state investigation
   Regulation: Pattern of non-compliance standard

IMPORTANT NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- These are BRIGHT-LINE rules - no judgment needed
- Any violation = automatic rejection recommendation
- Document which specific rule(s) were violated
- Cite the regulation in your reasoning

For all OTHER aspects (pricing reasonability, coverage quality, risk assessment, etc.) - use your expert judgment freely.
